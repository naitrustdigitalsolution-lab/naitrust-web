# Domain Glossary

## Safe Deal

A structured transaction between parties with agreed terms, evidence, protected payment status, and completion/dispute flow.

## Transaction Room

The central workspace for a safe deal. It shows parties, terms, payment status, milestones, evidence, activity, disputes, and allowed actions.

## Protected Payment

A payment coordinated through a regulated partner. The UI must not imply Naitrust directly holds customer funds unless legally approved.

## Protected Funding

The Phase 1 user-facing flow where the buyer funds a partner-issued virtual account dedicated to the transaction.

## Virtual Account

A bank account issued by the licensed partner for one transaction. The buyer sends funds there; Naitrust does not hold those funds.

## TransactionType

A backend configuration that decides the verification level, evidence requirements, release rules, dispute rules, and fee model for a safe deal.

## Auto-Confirm Window

The agreed period after seller evidence submission where the buyer must confirm or dispute. If the buyer is silent and rules are satisfied, release may be approved.

## Custody Boundary

The rule that Naitrust controls workflow and instructions, while a licensed partner holds and moves money.

## Verification

Proof that a user or business is legitimate enough for the requested risk level. Verification may include email, phone, ID, CAC, ownership, and documents.

## Liveness

A freshness check proving the verified person is currently present. Liveness can be required even when identity verification is still valid.

## Ownership Proof

Evidence that a verified individual controls or represents a verified business.

## Evidence

Documents, photos, receipts, waybills, invoices, inspection reports, contracts, messages, or other proof attached to a transaction.

## Dispute

A structured disagreement about transaction delivery, quality, payment release, refund, or terms.

## Reputation

Trust history generated from completed Naitrust transactions, not unsupported marketing claims.

## AI Assessment

An AI-generated summary, risk signal, evidence checklist, or recommendation. It is advisory and must not be treated as a final decision.
